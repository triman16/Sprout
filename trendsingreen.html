Job of the week: ESG Analyst

An ESG analyst is a professional who evaluates the environmental, social, and governance performance of companies and investments. ESG analysts help investors and businesses make informed decisions that align with their values and goals for sustainability.

Skills and qualifications

  • Non-traditional route: A bachelor's degree in any field, preferably with some exposure to finance, economics, or statistics. A strong interest and knowledge of sustainability issues and trends. Analytical, research, and communication skills. Ability to work with data and tools such as Excel, Bloomberg, and ESG rating platforms.
  • Traditional route: A bachelor's degree in finance, accounting, economics, or a related field. A master's degree or a professional certification such as CFA, FRM, or CAIA is preferred. Experience in financial analysis, valuation, or portfolio management. Knowledge of ESG frameworks and standards such as GRI, SASB, and TCFD.
