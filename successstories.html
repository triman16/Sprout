Saahas's Journey

Saahas is a non-profit organization that promotes waste management practices in alignment with the Municipal Solid Wastes (Management and Handling) Rules, 2000. Founded by Wilma Rodrigues, a former journalist, in 2001, Saahas has been helping build communities across rural and urban India that manage their waste at source by reducing, reusing and recycling their waste and achieving 90% resource recovery.

Saahas also evolved into a for-profit business model under the brand name of Saahas Zero Waste in 2013, which is backed by Indian Angel Networks and Upaya Social Ventures. Saahas Zero Waste provides end-to-end waste management solutions to corporates, institutions, residential complexes and events. It also creates livelihood opportunities for waste pickers and other marginalized groups.

Saahas has been recognized with several awards and accolades, such as the Namma Bengaluru Award, the Parivartan Sustainability Leadership Award, the Google Impact Challenge Award, and the Ashden Award. Saahas has also been featured in various media outlets, such as The Hindu, The Times of India, The Economic Times, and YourStory.

Switcheko's Journey

Switcheko is a platform that enables people to trade their waste for eco-friendly products. It aims to create a circular economy where waste is not wasted, but transformed into valuable resources. Switcheko also educates and empowers people to adopt a sustainable lifestyle and reduce their environmental footprint.

Switcheko was founded by Akshay Deshpande, an ecological entrepreneur, in 2018. He was inspired by his own experience of living in a zero-waste community in Auroville, where he learned about the importance of conscious choices and lasting impact. He wanted to bring the same concept to the urban population and make sustainability accessible and affordable for everyone.

Switcheko operates through an online platform and offline events, where people can exchange their waste for coins and redeem them for eco-friendly products, such as bamboo toothbrushes, organic soaps, cloth bags, and solar lamps. Switcheko also conducts training and awareness programs, transformation projects, community engagement activities, and green audits for corporates, schools, colleges, and other institutions.

Switcheko has partnered with various self-help groups, tribes, and social enterprises to source their products and support their livelihoods. Switcheko has also received recognition from various dignitaries, such as the Vice President of India, the Minister of State for UK, the Minister of Tourism for India, and the Minister for Telangana. Switcheko has also been featured in media outlets, such as The Hindu, The Times of India, and The New Indian Express.

Free up's Journey

Free up is a platform that allows people to sell and buy clothes, beauty and home items that are new or preloved. It aims to promote a circular fashion economy where people can reuse, recycle, and upcycle their items and reduce the waste and pollution caused by the fast fashion industry.

Free up was founded by a team of passionate entrepreneurs who wanted to create a social impact with their business. They realized that the fashion industry is one of the most polluting and wasteful industries in the world, and that there is a huge potential to save resources and money by extending the life cycle of clothes and other items.

Free up operates through an online platform and a mobile app, where people can list their items for sale or browse through the available items for purchase. Free up also offers doorstep delivery, payment protection, and quality check for all transactions. Free up also works with the self-help groups to provide them with the best products and support their income generation.

Free up has been growing rapidly since its launch in 2020, and has attracted thousands of users and sellers across India. Free up has also been featured in media outlets, such as The Economic Times, The Hindu Business Line, and YourStory.
